# GRE Quant — Full Course Map (cold start → max score)


A 5-week cold-start sprint to the maximum attainable Quant score, front-loaded so the foundation carries everything else. Weeks 1-2 rebuild number sense and the algebra/arithmetic mechanics that every later topic depends on, plus the calculator-light habits and QC plug-in strategy that protect accuracy. Weeks 2-3 drill the highest-frequency, highest-ROI workhorses (percents, ratios/rates, word-problem translation, linear/quadratic, core geometry, basic stats and probability) to lock in a strong Section 1, which is the single lever that unlocks Section 2's high-scoring ceiling. Week 4 adds the harder 170-band topics (advanced counting/probability, standard deviation and the normal curve, 3D, data-interpretation under the clock, layered QC), and Week 5 is pure simulation: full adaptive sections, official POWERPREP, relentless error-log review, and test-day pacing rehearsal.


## Weekly focus


**Week 1 — Number sense and arithmetic core**  
Rebuild mental math (fractions/decimals/percents, squares, roots, divisibility), master integer properties and exponents/roots, and learn the calculator-light habit. End the week fluent in the arithmetic that 60%+ of the test rests on.


**Week 2 — Algebra mechanics and the QC mindset**  
Get reliable at simplifying, linear/quadratic equations, inequalities, and translating words to equations; learn the Quantitative Comparison plug-in method (test 0, 1, negative, fraction). End able to solve a clean Section-1-level mixed set accurately, even if slowly.


**Week 3 — Geometry and core data analysis at speed**  
Cover all tested geometry (triangles, special right triangles, circles, polygons, area/volume, coordinate geometry) and core stats (mean/median/mode, ratio/rate word problems, basic probability and counting). Begin enforcing the ~1.75 min/question clock. Target a clean, accurate Section 1.


**Week 4 — 170-ceiling topics and adaptive Section 1 protection**  
Master the hard differentiators: advanced combinations/probability, standard deviation, the normal distribution and 68-95-99.7 rule, 3D figures, multi-step data interpretation, and layered QC traps. Run first full adaptive simulations and tighten pacing.


**Week 5 — Simulation, official calibration, and test-day execution**  
Take official POWERPREP plus full adaptive sims, grind the error log and leeches to zero, rehearse two-pass pacing and never-blank guessing, and peak for the 2026-07-31 test. Recalibrate the score estimate against real ETS results.


## Lessons


### Foundation

- **L01** (Arithmetic) — Fractions, decimals, and benchmark conversions — _Fractions/decimals underlie percents, ratios, probability, and nearly every calculation; fluency here removes calculator dependence and saves seconds everywhere._  ✅ ready
- **L02** (Arithmetic) — Order of operations and signed-number arithmetic — _Every multi-step problem and the on-screen calculator both demand correct PEMDAS and sign handling; errors here silently corrupt otherwise-correct work._  ✅ ready
- **L02b** (Arithmetic) — Estimation and mental-math number sense — _Estimation is the fastest path to answers and the primary defense against the clock; strong estimators eliminate wrong choices instantly and verify exact answers._  ✅ ready
- **L03** (Arithmetic) — Integer properties and odd/even rules — _Integer-property logic powers a large share of QC and number-theory traps; knowing parity and integer behavior lets you reason instead of compute._  ✅ ready
- **L04** (Arithmetic) — Divisibility, factors, and multiples — _Divisibility shortcuts and factor reasoning are high-frequency and feed primes, remainders, GCF/LCM, and fraction reduction._  ✅ ready
- **L05** (Arithmetic) — Prime numbers and prime factorization — _Prime factorization is the master tool for divisibility, exponents, GCF/LCM, and counting-factor problems that recur at the top difficulty band._  ✅ ready
- **L06** (Arithmetic) — Remainders and modular reasoning — _Remainder questions are common QC/number-theory traps that punish brute force; pattern reasoning solves them fast._  ✅ ready
- **L07** (Arithmetic) — Exponents and roots: rules and estimation — _Exponent and root manipulation is foundational to algebra, scientific notation, and growth problems, and is a frequent QC battleground._  ✅ ready
- **L08** (Arithmetic) — Percents and percent change — _Percents are among the highest-frequency GRE topics and a top source of careless errors (percent of vs percent change vs successive change)._  ✅ ready
- **L09** (Arithmetic) — Ratios and proportions — _Ratio reasoning underpins rates, mixtures, similar figures, and many word problems; the part-to-whole pitfall is a classic trap._  ✅ ready
- **L10** (Arithmetic) — Absolute value and the number line — _Absolute value and number-line reasoning recur in inequalities and QC; the 'two cases' nature is a frequent oversight._  ✅ ready
- **L11** (Algebra) — Simplifying expressions and the laws of algebra — _Clean algebraic manipulation is the gateway to every equation, inequality, and function; sloppy simplification is a hidden error source._  ✅ ready
- **L12** (Algebra) — Linear equations and the plug-in/back-solve toolkit — _Solving one-variable linear equations is the most-used algebra skill, and back-solving from answer choices is a core GRE time-saver._  ·
- **L17** (Algebra) — Translating word problems into equations — _Translation is the bottleneck skill for percents, rates, ages, mixtures, and work problems; misreading is the #1 silent error._  ·
- **L18** (Multiple) — Quantitative Comparison strategy and plug-in discipline — _QC opens every Quant section and is the highest-ROI question type; the plug-in method (0, 1, negative, fraction) and the 'cannot be determined' instinct directly drive Section 1 accuracy._  ·

### Build

- **L13** (Algebra) — Factoring and the special products — _Factoring (GCF, quadratics, difference of squares, perfect squares) unlocks quadratics, simplification, and many QC shortcuts._  ·
- **L14** (Algebra) — Quadratic equations — _Quadratics appear directly and inside geometry/word problems; knowing factoring + the quadratic formula covers all cases._  ·
- **L15** (Algebra) — Inequalities (including the sign-flip and absolute-value cases) — _Inequalities are heavily tested and trap-rich (flipping the sign when multiplying by a negative); essential for QC reasoning._  ·
- **L16** (Algebra) — Simultaneous equations — _Two-variable systems appear in word problems and data setups; substitution/elimination plus the 'sum/difference trick' are time-savers._  ·
- **L19** (Arithmetic) — Rate, work, and average-speed problems — _Rate/work/distance problems are high-frequency word problems with a notorious average-speed trap (cannot just average the two speeds)._  ·
- **L20** (Arithmetic) — Sequences (arithmetic and geometric) — _Sequence questions reward formula knowledge and pattern-spotting and appear in both QC and numeric entry._  ·
- **L21** (Geometry) — Lines, angles, and parallel-line relationships — _Angle rules are the entry point to all geometry and a frequent quick-win; parallel-line angle relationships recur constantly._  ·
- **L22** (Geometry) — Triangles: properties and angle/side rules — _Triangles are the most-tested geometry figure and the basis for Pythagoras, special triangles, similarity, and much of coordinate geometry._  ·
- **L23** (Geometry) — Pythagorean theorem and common triples — _Pythagoras and its triples (3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17) are everywhere and recognizing them on sight saves real time._  ·
- **L24** (Geometry) — Special right triangles (45-45-90 and 30-60-90) — _The two special triangles unlock squares' diagonals, equilateral heights, and many circle/coordinate problems with no calculation._  ·
- **L25** (Geometry) — Area and perimeter of polygons — _Area/perimeter of triangles and quadrilaterals is high-frequency and feeds composite-figure and 3D problems._  ·
- **L26** (Geometry) — Quadrilaterals and polygon angle rules — _Quadrilateral properties and the interior/exterior-angle formulas are testable directly and support polygon and similarity questions._  ·
- **L27** (Geometry) — Circles: circumference, area, arcs, and sectors — _Circles are high-frequency and combine area, proportion (arc/sector), and angle reasoning, including inscribed angles._  ·
- **L28** (Algebra) — Coordinate geometry: lines, slope, and intercepts — _Coordinate geometry bridges algebra and geometry and is heavily tested via slope, line equations, and intersections._  ·
- **L29** (Algebra) — Distance and midpoint formulas — _Distance and midpoint complete coordinate geometry and tie directly back to Pythagoras and segment reasoning._  ·
- **L30** (Algebra) — Functions, relations, and function notation — _Function notation and 'made-up function' symbol problems appear regularly and reward careful substitution._  ·
- **L31** (Data Analysis) — Mean, median, mode, and weighted averages — _Descriptive-statistics questions are high-frequency, and the mean-vs-median trap and weighted-average reasoning are common._  ·
- **L32** (Data Analysis) — Basic probability and the counting bridge — _Single-event probability is the foundation for compound, conditional, and expected-value questions later this week and next._  ·
- **L33** (Data Analysis) — The multiplication principle and basic counting — _The fundamental counting principle underlies permutations, combinations, and probability denominators; it is the prerequisite to all advanced counting._  ·

### Advanced

- **L34** (Data Analysis) — Permutations — _Permutations are a 170-band differentiator; recognizing order-matters and handling repetition/restrictions is the skill gap at the top._  ·
- **L35** (Data Analysis) — Combinations — _Combinations are among the hardest, highest-value counting questions and pair with probability for compound events._  ·
- **L36** (Data Analysis) — Compound probability (independent and dependent events) — _Compound probability with and/or and with/without replacement is a frequent top-difficulty trap._  ·
- **L37** (Data Analysis) — Conditional probability — _Conditional probability is a genuine 170-ceiling topic that even strong test-takers miss when they ignore the reduced sample space._  ·
- **L38** (Data Analysis) — Venn diagrams and overlapping sets — _Two- and three-set overlap problems are common and trap students who double-count the intersections._  ·
- **L39** (Data Analysis) — Expected value — _Expected value combines probability and weighted averages and appears in higher-difficulty data questions._  ·
- **L40** (Data Analysis) — Range, standard deviation, and spread — _Standard deviation is a top differentiator; the GRE rarely makes you compute it but constantly tests conceptual spread and comparison._  ·
- **L41** (Data Analysis) — Quartiles, IQR, percentiles, and boxplots — _Quartiles/percentiles and boxplot reading are common data-interpretation components that reward precise definitions._  ·
- **L42** (Data Analysis) — Normal distribution and the 68-95-99.7 rule — _The normal curve and empirical rule are a high-band topic that appears as a clean quick-win once the rule is memorized._  ·
- **L43** (Data Analysis) — Data interpretation sets under the clock — _Multi-question DI sets (tables, bar/line/circle graphs, scatterplots) are guaranteed points and reward fast, accurate reading plus calculator discipline._  ·
- **L44** (Geometry) — Similar and congruent figures — _Similarity (proportional sides, squared area ratio) is a high-difficulty geometry differentiator that traps students on area scaling._  ·
- **L45** (Geometry) — 3D figures: surface area and volume — _Solids (boxes, cylinders, the occasional sphere/cone) are testable and combine area, Pythagoras (space diagonal), and similarity._  ·
- **L46** (Multiple) — Advanced QC traps and select-all-that-apply — _Layered QC edge cases and the no-partial-credit select-all type are the most common top-band score-killers and demand a slower, exhaustive check._  ·

### TestPrep

- **L47** (Multiple) — First full adaptive simulation and pacing audit — _Section 1 accuracy sets Section 2's ceiling, so a realistic adaptive run plus a per-question time audit is the highest-leverage diagnostic of readiness._  ·
- **L48** (Multiple) — Official POWERPREP test and readiness recalibration — _Generated problems cannot perfectly match ETS difficulty, so an official POWERPREP score is the source of truth for the real score band._  ·
- **L49** (Multiple) — Error-log and leech eradication — _Closing the specific, repeated mistakes (leeches) is the fastest remaining point-gain in the final week._  ·
- **L50** (Multiple) — Timed mixed endurance and pacing peak — _Full back-to-back timed sections build the stamina and pacing instincts needed to sustain accuracy across all 27 questions on test day._  ·
- **L51** (Multiple) — Test-day strategy and execution rehearsal — _A rehearsed test-day routine (AWA energy budget, two-pass per section, calculator discipline, never-blank guessing) prevents avoidable point loss when it counts._  ·
